Who are we?

The Mbondo tribe, also known as the Mbembe and the Babembe, lives predominantly in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC). It is essential to note that Africa is home to numerous ethnic groups, each with its own culture, history, and traditions. The Bembe tribe is just one of the many diverse and intriguing ethnic groups that contribute to the cultural richness of the African continent.

What is our mission?

Our mission is to help the elderly and young children who can't help themselves on the street and in their homes. We collaborate with existing organizations and local authorities to ensure our efforts align with the community's requirements and to maximize our impact. Together, we can build a society that becomes more inclusive and compassionate towards those in need.

How do we accomplish our mission?

We advocate for enhanced social services and support systems for the elderly and vulnerable children with local authorities and community leaders. By increasing awareness of the obstacles they face, we aim to influence policies and resources that effectively address their needs. We provide practical assistance to those in need, such as food, clothing, and shelter. Additionally, we offer help with domestic chores, repairs, or simply companionship and emotional support.

Where and how do we run our business?

We are operational in twelve Irish counties, including Carlow, Cavan, Clare, Cork, Donegal, Dublin, Galway, Kerry, Kildare, Kilkenny, Laois, and Monaghan.

Organizations Tree

  • Board of Directors

    • Chairman

    • CEO/Executive Director

      The CEO or Executive Director oversees managing and directing the entire organisation. Together with the board, they create and implement long-term plans, oversee day-to-day operations, and ensure the organization's mission and objectives are achieved and maintained.

      • Management Team

        The Management Team works with the CEO to make sure the organisation runs smoothly and comes up with plans for how to reach our goal. They have many groups that work together to make sure everything runs smoothly.

        • Programs/Services Department

          To meet the needs of people all over Ireland, the Programs/Services Department plans, carries out, and evaluates programmes and services. To do this, they have to figure out what the community needs, make programmes that meet those needs, and then figure out how well those programmes worked.

          • Program Manager

            While working with teams to make sure the programmes are carried out successfully, programme managers are in charge of those programmes. The programme leaders decide what the goals are, how to use the resources available, and how well the programme is doing.

          • Program Coordinator

            Coordinators of programmes help Programme Managers run and coordinate programmes all over Ireland. They work together with partners, staff, and volunteers to make sure the programme is a success.

        • Support Staff

          In our organisation, the Support Staff helps different areas with both operational and administrative tasks. They are in charge of things like managing data, making schedules, and keeping the office in good shape.

          • Finance

            The Finance department is in charge of our company's money and makes sure it can stay in business. Their job is to make budgets, do accounts, report on finances, and raise money from the government, our members, and charitable individuals everywhere.

          • Human Resources

            Concerns about employees are handled by the Human Resources department. In this role, they are responsible for hiring people, teaching them, managing their work, and making sure they follow HR rules and regulations regarding labour.

      • Volunteers

        An overwhelming majority of our Volunteers give their time and skills to help the group's events and programmes. In addition to helping staff and beneficiaries, they may act as teachers, assistants, or advocates.

  • Beneficiaries/Vulnerable Individuals

    most of all People who benefit from this help are mmbondo communities, which live in all of Ireland's counties, as well as other minority ethnic groups that live in Ireland and the communities that the organisation helps by offering a range of support services.
